# Economics

## What a Converted Lead is Worth to Credex

Credex sells discounted AI credits. Assume:
- Average credit purchase: $2,000 (one quarter of AI spend for a small team)
- Gross margin on discounted credits: 15-25%
- Gross profit per transaction: $300-500
- Repeat purchase rate: 60% quarterly

**Lifetime value of one converted customer:**
- Year 1: 3 purchases × $400 average gross profit = $1,200
- With 60% retention into Year 2: $720
- Simple 2-year LTV: ~$1,900

A converted lead — someone who books a consultation and buys — is worth approximately $1,500-2,000 to Credex over 2 years.

## Customer Acquisition Cost by Channel

| Channel | Estimated CAC | Reasoning |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Hacker News Show HN | $0 direct, ~$200 time cost | Organic, one post reaches 5,000-50,000 relevant users |
| Reddit posts | $0 direct, ~$100 time cost | 2-3 posts per week, each reaching 1,000-10,000 users |
| Twitter direct outreach | $0 direct, ~$150 time cost | 20 targeted replies per week |
| Warm Credex partner intros | $0 direct, relationship cost | Highest quality leads, lowest friction |

If the tool converts 1 in 50 audits to a Credex consultation booking, and 1 in 3 consultations converts to a purchase:

- CAC = cost to get 150 audits completed
- At zero paid budget, 150 audits costs roughly $500 in founder time
- CAC ≈ $500 per paying customer
- LTV:CAC ratio ≈ 3:1 — acceptable for a B2B SaaS lead gen tool

## Conversion Funnel

```
Cold visitor lands on page
        ↓ 40% (industry benchmark for free tools with clear value prop)
Audit completed
        ↓ 20% (email captured — value shown first, friction low)
Lead captured
        ↓ 15% (high savings case — >$500/month flagged for Credex outreach)
Consultation booked
        ↓ 30% (consultation to purchase — Credex sales conversion)
Credit purchase made
```

**Net conversion: audit → purchase = 0.20 × 0.15 × 0.30 = 0.9%**

This means roughly 1 in 110 audits becomes a paying Credex customer.

## What Makes This Profitable

At $1,500 LTV and $500 CAC, the tool is profitable at current assumptions.

The tool becomes significantly more profitable when:
- Shareable URLs drive organic audits at zero marginal cost
- Credex partner distribution delivers warm leads with higher conversion rates
- The audit result email nurtures leads over time without additional spend

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months

**Revenue model:** Credex earns gross profit on credit sales, not SaaS subscriptions.

**The math:**
- $1M ARR in gross profit ÷ $1,500 LTV per customer = 667 paying customers needed
- 667 customers ÷ 18 months = 37 new customers per month
- At 0.9% audit-to-purchase conversion = 4,100 audits per month needed
- At 40% visitor-to-audit conversion = 10,250 monthly visitors needed

**10,250 monthly visitors in 18 months is achievable if:**
- Hacker News post drives 2,000-5,000 visitors in week 1
- Shareable audit URLs generate compounding organic traffic
- 2-3 Reddit posts per month each drive 500-1,000 visitors
- SEO on "AI tool cost comparison" queries builds over 6-12 months

**What has to be true:**
1. Consultation booking rate stays at 15% of leads — requires fast Credex follow-up on flagged leads
2. Sales conversion stays at 30% — requires Credex to have competitive credit pricing
3. Organic sharing drives at least 30% of audits — requires the shareable URL to be genuinely useful

**Biggest risk:** The funnel breaks if Credex follow-up on high-savings leads is slow. A founder who sees $500/month in savings will evaluate alternatives within 48 hours. If Credex does not reach out within 24 hours of a flagged lead, the conversion opportunity is likely lost.
