# Go To Market

## Exact Target User

Not "startups." The specific person is:

**CTO or Engineering Manager at a seed-to-Series-A startup, 3-15 people, using 3+ AI tools, paying $200-2000/month across subscriptions they set up one by one over 18 months and never audited.**

They are not a finance person. They bought these tools because they needed them at the time. They have no process for reviewing SaaS spend. They feel vague guilt about the bill but never act on it because there is no obvious trigger and no benchmark to compare against.

## What They Google Before Wanting This Tool

- "cursor vs github copilot 2025"
- "is chatgpt team worth it for small team"
- "claude pro vs team plan difference"
- "how much should a startup spend on AI tools"
- "reduce SaaS costs startup"

These are not searches for an audit tool. They are searches that happen right before someone would want one. This is where content and SEO should intercept them.

## Where They Hang Out Online

- **r/ExperiencedDevs** and **r/startups** on Reddit — engineers discussing tooling decisions
- **Hacker News** — "Ask HN: what's your AI tool stack" threads get hundreds of comments
- **Indie Hackers** — founders openly discuss costs and margins
- **X (Twitter)** — developers who tweet about Cursor, Claude, Copilot are the exact audience
- **Slack communities** — Online Geniuses, Ramen Club, SaaS founders Slack groups
- **Discord** — Cursor Discord, Anthropic Discord, indie hacker servers

## First 100 Users in 30 Days — Zero Budget

**Week 1 — Direct outreach**
Post in Indie Hackers: "I built a free tool that audits your AI tool spend — here is what I found after testing it with 10 startups." Include a real audit result screenshot showing $400/month in savings. This gets clicks because it is specific and free.

**Week 2 — Hacker News Show HN**
Submit "Show HN: Free AI spend auditor for startups — finds overspend in 2 minutes." Time it for Tuesday or Wednesday morning US Eastern. The shareable URL feature means every upvoter can generate their own audit and share it.

**Week 3 — Twitter seeding**
Find 20 developers who have tweeted about paying for Cursor or Claude in the last 30 days. Reply to their tweets with the tool. Not spam — a direct reply saying "built this because of tweets like yours."

**Week 4 — Reddit**
Post in r/startups and r/ExperiencedDevs with a specific title: "I audited my AI tool spend and found I was paying $340/month more than I needed to — here is the free tool I built to do it." Personal story format, tool linked at the bottom.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex already has relationships with companies that overforecast AI credits — that is the core business. Those same companies have networks of startups they work with. A warm introduction from a Credex partner to 50 portfolio startups saying "use this free audit tool" is worth more than 10,000 cold impressions. No competitor can replicate a distribution channel built on existing credit supplier relationships.
